Task 2: Age-Appropriate Reading

Challenge reading Underline one benefit and circle one risk. Then answer the reading check.

Artificial intelligence, often shortened to AI, is technology that can carry out tasks that normally need human thinking. AI systems can look for patterns in data, make predictions, answer questions, recognise images, translate languages, recommend videos, or help people write and edit. They do this by following algorithms, which are step-by-step instructions or rules. Some AI systems learn from very large sets of data, so the quality of that data matters.

AI can bring clear benefits. It can help doctors spot signs of illness, support people with disabilities, translate information quickly, and help workers complete repeated tasks faster. In school, AI tools can suggest quiz questions, explain a difficult idea in a new way, or give feedback on a draft. Used well, AI can save time and help people access information. However, it should support human thinking, not replace it.

There are also risks. AI can make mistakes or invent information, which is sometimes called a hallucination. If the data used to train an AI system is unfair or incomplete, the AI may produce biased results. AI tools can also collect personal data, so privacy matters. Another risk is misinformation: realistic fake images, audio, or text can make it harder to know what is true. Responsible AI use means checking sources, protecting private information, being honest when AI has helped, and asking whether the output is fair, accurate and useful.

Reading Check

  1. Artificial intelligence.
  2. Examples: recognising images, translating languages, answering questions, recommending videos, helping write/edit, spotting patterns.
  3. Poor, unfair or incomplete data can lead to poor or biased AI outputs.
  4. A confident-sounding answer that is wrong or invented.
  5. Check sources, protect personal information, be honest about AI help, and check fairness and accuracy.

Supported Reading

Guided reading This shorter version uses the same ideas with simpler sentences.

AI means artificial intelligence. AI is computer technology that can do some tasks that need thinking. It can answer questions, translate words, recommend videos and help people write.

AI can be helpful. It can save time, explain hard ideas and help people find information. In school, AI can help with revision or give feedback, but students still need to think for themselves.

AI can also be risky. It can make mistakes, give unfair answers, or share false information. Students should protect private information, check facts, and be honest when AI has helped them.

  3. It can save time or explain hard ideas.
  4. It can make mistakes, be unfair or share false information.
  5. Check facts, protect private information and be honest about using AI.
